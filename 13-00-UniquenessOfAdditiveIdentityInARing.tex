\begin{lemma}
Let $R$ be a ring. There exists a unique element $0$ in $R$ such that for all $a$ in $R$:
$$0+a=a+0=a.$$
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By the definition of ring, there exists at least one \PMlinkescapeword{additive} identity in $R$, call it $0_1$. Suppose $0_2\in R$ is an element which also \PMlinkescapeword{satisfies} the \PMlinkescapeword{property} of additive identity. Thus, 
$$0_2+0_1=0_2$$
On the other hand, $0_1$ is an additive identity, therefore:
$$0_2+0_1=0_1+0_2=0_1$$
Hence $0_2=0_1$, i.e. there is a unique additive identity.
\end{proof}
