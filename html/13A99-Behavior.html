behavior


If R is an infinite cyclic ring (http://planetmath.org/CyclicRing3), the behavior of R is a nonnegative integer k such that there exists a generator (http://planetmath.org/Generator) r of the additive group of R with r2=k⁢r.

If R is a finite cyclic ring of order n, the behavior of R is a positive divisor k of n such that there exists a generator r of the additive group of R with r2=k⁢r.

For any cyclic ring, behavior exists uniquely. Moreover, the behavior of a cyclic ring determines many of its .
