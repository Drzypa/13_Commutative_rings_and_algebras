homogeneous system of parameters


Let k be a field, let R be an ℕm-graded (http://planetmath.org/GradedAlgebra) k-algebra, and let M be a ℤm-graded R-module.

Let ℋ⁢(R+) be the homogeneous union of the irrelevant ideal of R.

A partial homogeneous system of parameters for M is a finite sequence of elements θ1,θ2,…,θr∈ℋ⁢(R+) such that

dim⁡(M/(∑i=1rθi⁢M))=dim⁡(M)-r,

where dim gives the Krull dimension.

A () homogeneous system of parameters is a partial homogeneous system of parameters such that r=dim⁡(M).

A sequence θ1,…,θr∈ℋ⁢(R+) is a M-sequence if for all i with 0≤i<r, we have that θi+1 is not a zero-divisor in

M/(∑j=1iθi⁢M).

Finally, view M as being ℤ-graded by using any specialization of the above ℤm-grading. Then we define the depth of M to be the length of the longest homogeneous M-sequence.
