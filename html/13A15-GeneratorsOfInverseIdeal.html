generators of inverse ideal


Theorem.  Let R be a commutative ring with non-zero unity and let T be the total ring of fractions of R.  If  𝔞=(a1,…,an)  is an invertible fractional ideal (http://planetmath.org/FractionalIdealOfCommutativeRing) of R with  𝔞⁢𝔟=R,  then also the inverse ideal 𝔟 can be generated by n elements of T.

Proof.  The equation  𝔞⁢𝔟=(1)  implies the existence of the elements ai′ of 𝔞 and bi′ of 𝔟  (i=1,…,m) such that  a1′⁢b1′+⋯+am′⁢bm′=1.  Because the ai′’s are in 𝔞, they may be expressed as

ai′=∑j=1nri⁢jaj  (i=1,…,m),

where the ri⁢j’s are some elements of R.  Now the unity acquires the form

1=∑i=1mai′⁢bi′=∑i=1m∑j=1nri⁢j⁢aj⁢bi′=∑j=1naj⁢∑i=1mri⁢j⁢bi′=∑j=1naj⁢bj,

in which

bj=∑i=1mri⁢jbi′∈R𝔟=𝔟  (j=1,…,n).

Thus an arbitrary element b of the 𝔟 satisfies the condition

b=b⋅1=∑j=1n(aj⁢b)⁢bj∈R⁢b1+⋯+R⁢bn=(b1,…,bn).

Consequently,  𝔟⊆(b1,…,bn).  Since the inverse inclusion is apparent, we have the equality

𝔞-1=𝔟=(b1,…,bn).
