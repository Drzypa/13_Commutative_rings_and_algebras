Theorem (Eisenstein criterion).

Let f be a primitive polynomial over a commutative unique factorization domain R, say

f⁢(x)=a0+a1⁢x+a2⁢x2+…+an⁢xn.

If R has an irreducible element p such that

p∣am  0≤m≤n-1
p2∤a0
p∤an

then f is irreducible.

Proof.

Suppose

f=(b0+…+bs⁢xs)⁢(c0+…+ct⁢xt)

where s>0 and t>0. Since a0=b0⁢c0, we know that p divides one but not both of b0 and c0; suppose p∣c0. By hypothesis, not all the cm are divisible by p; let k be the smallest index such that p∤ck. We have ak=b0⁢ck+b1⁢ck-1+…+bk⁢c0. We also have p∣ak, and p divides every summand except one on the right side, which yields a contradiction. QED ∎
