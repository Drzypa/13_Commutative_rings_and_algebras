polynomial ring over integral domain


Theorem.

If the coefficient ring R is an integral domain, then so is also its polynomial ring R⁢[X].

Proof.  Let f⁢(X) and g⁢(X) be two non-zero polynomials in R⁢[X] and let af and bg be their leading coefficients, respectively.  Thus  af≠0,  bg≠0,  and because R has no zero divisors,  af⁢bg≠0.  But the product af⁢bg is the leading coefficient of f⁢(X)⁢g⁢(X) and so f⁢(X)⁢g⁢(X) cannot be the zero polynomial.  Consequently, R⁢[X] has no zero divisors, Q.E.D.

Remark.  The theorem may by induction be generalized for the polynomial ring  R⁢[X1,X2,…,Xn].
