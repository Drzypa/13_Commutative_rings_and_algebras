cyclic rings of behavior one


Theorem.

A cyclic ring has a multiplicative identity if and only if it has behavior one.

Proof.

For a proof that a cyclic ring with a multiplicative identity has behavior one, see this theorem (http://planetmath.org/MultiplicativeIdentityOfACyclicRingMustBeAGenerator).

Let R be a cyclic ring with behavior one. Let r be a generator (http://planetmath.org/Generator) of the additive group of R such that r2=r. Let s∈R. Then there exists a∈R with s=a⁢r. Since r⁢s=r⁢(a⁢r)=a⁢r2=a⁢r=s and multiplication in cyclic rings is commutative, then r is a multiplicative identity. ∎
