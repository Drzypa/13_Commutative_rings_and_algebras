cancellation ideal


Let R be a commutative ring containing regular elements and 𝔖 be the multiplicative semigroup of the non-zero fractional ideals of R.  A fractional ideal 𝔞 of R is called a cancellation ideal or simply cancellative, if it is a cancellative element of 𝔖, i.e. if

𝔞⁢𝔟=𝔞⁢𝔠⇒𝔟=𝔠 ∀𝔟,𝔠∈𝔖.
  • •

    Each invertible ideal is cancellative.

  • •

    A finite product 𝔞1⁢𝔞2⁢…⁢𝔞m of fractional ideals is cancellative iff every 𝔞i is such.

  • •

    The fractional ideal  𝔞/r:={a⁢r-1:a∈𝔞},  where 𝔞 is an integral ideal of R and r a regular element of R, is cancellative if and only if 𝔞 is cancellative in the multiplicative semigroup of the non-zero integral ideals of R.

  • •

    If  r∈R,  then the principal ideal (r) of R is cancellative if and only if r is a regular element of the total ring of fractions of R.

  • •

    If  𝔞1+𝔞2+…+𝔞m  is a cancellation ideal and n a positive integer, then

    (𝔞1+𝔞2+…+𝔞m)n=𝔞1n+𝔞2n+…+𝔞mn.

    In particular, if the ideal  (a1,a2,…,am)  of R is cancellative, then

    (a1,a2,…,am)n=(a1n,a2n,…,amn).
