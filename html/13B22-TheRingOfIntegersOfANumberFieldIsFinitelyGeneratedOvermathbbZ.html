the ring of integers of a number field is finitely generated over ℤ


Theorem.

Let K be a number field of degree n over Q and let OK be the ring of integers of K. The ring OK is a free abelian group of rank n. In other words, there exists a finite integral basis (with n elements) for K, i.e. there exist algebraic integers α1,…,αn such that every element of OK can be expressed uniquely as a Z-linear combination of the αi.

Corollary.

Every ideal of OK is finitely generated.

Proof of the corollary.

By the theorem, 𝒪K is a free abelian group of rank n, and therefore it is finitely generated. Notice that an ideal is an additive subgroup. Finally a subgroup of a finitely generated free abelian group is also finitely generated. ∎

This is the first step to prove that 𝒪K is a Dedekind domain. Notice that the field of fractions of 𝒪K is the field K itself. Therefore, by definition, 𝒪K is integrally closed in K.
