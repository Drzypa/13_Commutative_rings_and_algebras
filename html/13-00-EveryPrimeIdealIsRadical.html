every prime ideal is radical


Let ℛ be a commutative ring and let 𝔓 be a prime ideal of ℛ.

Proposition 1.

Every prime ideal P of R is a radical ideal, i.e.

𝔓=Rad⁢(𝔓)
Proof.

Recall that 𝔓⊊ℛ is a prime ideal if and only if for any a,b∈ℛ

a⋅b∈𝔓⇒a∈𝔓⁢ or ⁢b∈𝔓

Also, recall that

Rad⁡(𝔓)={r∈ℛ∣∃n∈ℕ⁢ such that ⁢rn∈𝔓}

Obviously, we have 𝔓⊆Rad⁡(𝔓) (just take n=1), so it remains to show the reverse inclusion.

Suppose r∈Rad⁡(𝔓), so there exists some n∈ℕ such that rn∈𝔓. We want to prove that r must be an element of the prime ideal 𝔓. For this, we use induction on n to prove the following proposition:

For all n∈ℕ, for all r∈ℛ, rn∈𝔓⇒r∈𝔓.

Case n=1: This is clear, r∈𝔓⇒r∈𝔓.

Case n ⇒ Case n+1: Suppose we have proved the proposition for the case n, so our induction hypothesis is

∀r∈ℛ,rn∈𝔓⇒r∈𝔓

and suppose rn+1∈𝔓. Then

r⋅rn=rn+1∈𝔓

and since 𝔓 is a prime ideal we have

r∈𝔓⁢ or ⁢rn∈𝔓

Thus we conclude, either directly or using the induction hypothesis, that r∈𝔓 as desired.

∎
