radical of an integer


Given a natural number n, let n=p1α1⁢⋯⁢pkαk be its unique factorization as a product of distinct prime powers. Define the of n, denoted rad⁢(n), to be the product p1⁢⋯⁢pk. The radical of a square-free number is itself.
