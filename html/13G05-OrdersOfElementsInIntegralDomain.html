orders of elements in integral domain


Theorem.

Let  (D,+,⋅)  be an integral domain, i.e. a commutative ring with non-zero unity 1 and no zero divisors.  All non-zero elements of D have the same order (http://planetmath.org/OrderGroup) in the additive group  (D,+).

Proof.  Let a be arbitrary non-zero element.  Any multiple (http://planetmath.org/GeneralAssociativity) n⁢a may be written as

n⁢a=n⁢(1⁢a)=1⁢a+1⁢a+⋯+1⁢a⏟n=(1+1+⋯+1⏟n)⁢a=(n⁢1)⁢a.

Thus, because  a≠0  and there are no zero divisors, an equation  n⁢a=0  is equivalent (http://planetmath.org/Equivalent3) with the equation  n⁢1=0.  So a must have the same as the unity of D.

Note.  The of the unity element is the characteristic (http://planetmath.org/Characteristic) of the integral domain, which is 0 or a positive prime number.
