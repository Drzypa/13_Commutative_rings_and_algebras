Hensel’s lemma


The following results are used to show the existence of a solution to polynomial equations over local fields. Notice the similarities with Newton’s method.

Theorem (Hensel’s Lemma).

Let K be a local field (http://planetmath.org/LocalField), complete with respect to a valuation |⋅|. Let OK be the ring of integers in K (i.e. the set of elements of K with |k|≤1). Let f⁢(x) be a polynomial with coefficients in OK and suppose there exist α0∈OK such that

|f⁢(α0)|<|f′⁢(α0)2|.

Then there exist a root α∈K of f⁢(x). Moreover, the sequence:

αi+1=αi-f⁢(αi)f′⁢(αi)

converges to α. Furthermore:

|α-α0|≤|f⁢(αi)f′⁢(αi)|<1.
Corollary (Trivial case of Hensel’s lemma).

Let K be a number field and let p be a prime ideal in the ring of integers OK. Let Kp be the completion of K at the finite place p and let Op be the ring of integers in Kp. Let f⁢(x) be a polynomial with coefficients in Op and suppose there exist α0∈Op such that

f⁢(α0)≡0mod𝔭,f′⁢(α0)≠0mod𝔭.

Then there exist a root α∈Kp of f⁢(x), i.e. f⁢(α)=0.
