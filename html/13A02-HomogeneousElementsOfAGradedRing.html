homogeneous elements of a graded ring


Let k be a field, and let R be a connected commutative k-algebra graded (http://planetmath.org/GradedAlgebra) by ℕm. Then via the grading, we can decompose R into a direct sum of vector spaces: R=∐ω∈ℕmRω, where R0=k.

For an arbitrary ring element x∈R, we define the homogeneous degree of x to be the value ω such that x∈Rω, and we denote this by deg⁡(x)=ω. (See also homogeneous ideal)

A set of some importance (ironically), is the irrelevant ideal of R, denoted by R+, and given by

R+=∐ω≠0Rω.

Finally, we often need to consider the elements of such a ring R without using the grading, and we do this by looking at the homogeneous union of R:

ℋ⁢(R)=⋃ωRω.

In particular, in defining a homogeneous system of parameters, we are looking at elements of ℋ⁢(R+).
