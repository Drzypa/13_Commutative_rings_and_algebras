counter example to Nakayama’s lemma for non-finitely generated modules


The hypothesis that the module M be finitely generated is really necessary. For example, the field of p-adic numbers ℚp is not finitely generated over its ring of integers ℤp and (p)⁢ℚp=ℚp.

In one sense, the reason why ℚp is “bad” is that is has no proper sub module which is also maximal. Thus ℚp has no non-zero simple quotient. This explains why the following Proof of Nakayama’s Lemma (http://planetmath.org/ProofOfNakayamasLemma2) does not work for non-finitely generated modules.
