symmetric multilinear function


Let R be a commutative ring with identity and M,N be unital R-modules.

Suppose that ϕ:M×⋯×M→N is a multilinear map, where there are n copies of M.

Let H be a subgroup of Sn, the symmetric group on {1,…,n}, and χ:H→R satisfy

  1. 1.

    χ⁢(e)=1

  2. 2.

    χ⁢(g1⁢g2)=χ⁢(g1)⁢χ⁢(g2) for all g1,g2∈H

We say that ϕ is symmetric with respect to H and χ if

ϕ⁢(mσ⁢(1),…,mσ⁢(n))=χ⁢(σ)⁢ϕ⁢(m1,…,mn)

holds for all σ∈H and all mi∈M.

Now suppose that H=Sn.

If χ=1 then we say that ϕ is a symmetric multilinear function. If χ=ϵ, the sign of the permutation σ, we say that ϕ is a skew-symmetric multilinear function.

For example, the permanent is a symmetric multilinear function of its rows (columns).

The determinant is a skew-symmetric multilinear function of its rows (columns).
