proof of Dedekind domains with finitely many primes are PIDs


Proof.

Let 𝔭1,…,𝔭k be all the primes of a Dedekind domain R. If I is any ideal of R, then by the Weak Approximation Theorem we can choose x∈R such that ν𝔭i⁢((x))=ν𝔭i⁢(I) for all i (where ν𝔭 is the 𝔭-adic valuation). But since R is Dedekind, ideals have unique factorization; since (x) and I have identical factorizations, we must have (x)=I and I is principal. ∎
