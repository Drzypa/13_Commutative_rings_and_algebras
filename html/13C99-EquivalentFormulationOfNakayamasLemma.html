equivalent formulation of Nakayama’s lemma


The following is equivalent to Nakayama’s lemma.

Let A be a ring, M be a finitely-generated A-module, N a submodule of M, and 𝔞 an ideal of A contained in its Jacobson radical. Then M=𝔞⁢M+N⇒M=N.

Clearly this statement implies Nakayama’s Lemma, by setting N to 0. To see that it follows from Nakayama’s Lemma, note first that by the second isomorphism theorem for modules,

𝔞⁢M+NN=𝔞⁢M𝔞⁢M∩N

and the obvious map

𝔞⁢M→𝔞⁢MN:a⁢m↦a⁢(m+N)

is surjective; the kernel is clearly 𝔞⁢M∩N. Thus

𝔞⁢M+NN≅𝔞⁢MN

So from M=𝔞⁢M+N we get M/N=𝔞⁢(M/N). Since 𝔞 is contained in the Jacobson radical of M, it is contained in the Jacobson radical of M/N, so by Nakayama, M/N=0, i.e. M=N.
